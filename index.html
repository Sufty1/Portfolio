About Me

I'm a sophomore at Duke University studying Electrical and Computer Engineering, with a focus on building and understanding complex systems — from embedded hardware to large-scale infrastructure.

Born in Mexico and raised in the U.S. since age 14, I bring a perspective shaped by adaptability, discipline, and curiosity. My work spans hands-on engineering and research, including developing microrobotics systems and designing hardware/software projects that integrate computation with the physical world.

I'm especially interested in how engineering can power the next generation of technologies in energy, robotics, and beyond.

Projects

001 Microrobotics

MXene Vapor-Propelled Microrobot

Developed an untethered microrobot using MXene-based composite films and laser-induced vapor propulsion. Designed and tested propulsion mechanisms, identifying asymmetric vapor jetting as a controllable method for rotational flight and stability.

Material Fabrication NIR Laser Systems Experimental Testing Data Analysis
002 Digital Logic

16-bit RISC CPU

Engineered a single-cycle 16-bit RISC processor with a custom instruction set, including ALU, control unit, and register file. Implemented and debugged assembly-level programs such as Fibonacci sequence generation and prime detection.

Logisim Evolution Assembly Digital Logic Design
003 Embedded Systems

Autonomous CX-Bot

Designed and programmed an autonomous robot capable of navigation, sensing, and communication. Integrated sensors and wireless modules to enable coordinated movement and real-time data exchange between units.

Arduino C / C++ XBee QTI Sensors
004 Algorithms

Highway Route Finder
